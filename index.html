About

Hi, my name is Heather and I am a second year Computer Science student at the University of California, Santa Barbara. I am highly motivated and determined, and my biggest passion is discovering new hobbies that I enjoy and obsessively working to improve my skills. Whether it be delving into areas of software I've never encountered before (always with Stack Overflow open in another tab), absorbing an overwhelming amount of networking advice, or even finding a new way to bake banana bread, I am always eager to learn!

Currently, I am an Undergraduate Researcher at the Washburn Lab in UCSB's Marine Science Institute. As a developer, I'm building a more mobile-friendly web application to display vector overlays of ocean currents and manipulate map features for field researchers across the nation. In addition, I am a part of the Avionic Team of the Rocket Propulsion Laboratory at UCSB. My projects include a native desktop GUI application that will allow users to view and send commands to the rocket, such as altering the state of the actuators. In the future, I will be a Software Engineer Intern at ServiceNow for the summer of 2021!

Aside from academic and professional experiences, I love being involved with computer science education and supporting other women in tech. I am currently serving as the Treasurer for UCSB's chapter of the Society of Women Engineers (encouraging women to explore the engineering industry, as well as outreach to show younger kids how fun engineering can be!), and was previously an Undergraduate Learning Assistant for an intermediate programming course at UCSB (Intro to C++, assisted during lab hours to give students a better understanding of the core principles of data structures and algorithms).

As mentioned before, I love picking up new hobbies! I enjoy longboarding, playing sports (tennis, basketball, volleyball), playing music (piano, guitar), and baking the occasional banana bread.
